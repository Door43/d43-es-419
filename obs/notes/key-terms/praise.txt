====== praise //__alabanza__ //  ======

==== (praise, to praise) ====

**<font 16px/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>(alabanza, alabar)</font>    **

===== Definition: =====

**Definición:**

To praise someone is to express admiration and honor for that person.

**Alabar a alguien es expresarle admiración <font 16px/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>y honor a</font>    esa <font 16px/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>persona</font>    <font 16px/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>.</font>    **
  * People praise God because of how great he is and because of all the amazing things he has done as the Creator and Savior of the world.
  * **La gente alaba a Dios por su grandeza y por todas las cosas maravillosas que Él ha hecho como Creador y Salvador del mundo.**
  * Praise for God often includes being thankful for what he has done.
  * **La alabanza a Dios incluye ser agradecido por las cosas que Él ha hecho.**
  * Music and singing is often used as a way to praise God.
  * **La mú​sica y el canto a menudo son usadas como una forma de alabar a Dios.**
  * "Praise" that is directed to God is often a part of "worship."
  * **La "alabanza" que va dirigida a Dios a menudo es parte del "culto****".**
[See also: **[[:es-419:obs:notes:key-terms:worship|worship]]**]

===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:12-13|[12-13]]]** The Israelites sang many songs to celebrate their new freedom and to **praise**  God because he saved them from the Egyptian army.
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:17-08|[17-08]]]** When David heard these words, he immediately thanked and **praised**  God because he had promised David this great honor and many blessings.
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:22-07|[22-07]]]** Zechariah said, "**Praise**  God, because he has remembered his people!
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:43-13|[43-13]]]** They (disciples) enjoyed **praising**  God together and they shared everything they had with each other.
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:47-08|[47-08]]]** They put Paul and Silas in the most secure part of the prison and even locked up their feet. Yet in the middle of the night, they were singing songs of **praise**  to God.
