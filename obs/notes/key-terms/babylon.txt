====== Babylon Babilonia B ======

==== (Babylon, Babylonian) ====

(Babilonia, babilonio)

===== Facts: =====

**Datos:**

The ancient city of Babylon was located along the Euphrates River. Babylon was the capital of the Babylonian Empire which was located in the region of what is now the country of Iraq.

**La ciudad antigua de Babilonia estaba localizada a lo largo del Río Eufrates. Babilonia era la capital del Imperio Babilónico que estaba localizado en la región de lo que es hoy el paìs de Irak.**

   * Babylon was located in the same region where the Tower of Babel had been built hundreds of years before.
  * **Babilonia estaba localizada en la misma región donde estaba la Torre de Babel que fue construída cientos de años atrás.**
  * The Babylonians were a powerful people group that attacked the kingdom of Judah and held some of them captive for 70 years.
  * **Los Babilonios era gente bien poderosa que atacaron al reino de Judá y mantuvieron algunos de ellos cautivos por 70 años.**
  * Sometimes the word "Babylon" is used to refer to the entire Babylonian Empire. The "king of Babylon" was king not only of that city, but of the whole empire.
  * **A veces la palabra " Babilonia" es usada para referirse a todo el Imperio Babilónico". El "rey de Babilonia" no era solamente rey de esa ciudad, sino de todo el imperio.**
[See also: **[[:es-419:obs:notes:key-terms:kingdom-of-judah|kingdom of Judah]]**, **[[:es-419:obs:notes:key-terms:nebuchadnezzar|Nebuchadnezzar]]**]

**Vea tambien: reino de Judah, Nebuchadnezzar**

===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:20-06|[20-06]]]** About 100 years after the Assyrians destroyed the kingdom of Israel, God sent Nebuchadnezzar, king of the **Babylonians**, to attack the kingdom of Judah. **Babylon**  was a powerful empire.
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:20-07|[20-07]]]** But after a few years, the king of Judah rebelled against **Babylon**. So, the **Babylonians**  came back and attacked the kingdom of Judah. They captured the city of Jerusalem, destroyed the Temple, and took away all the treasures of the city and the Temple.
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:20-09|[20-09]]]** Nebuchadnezzar and his army took almost all of the people of the kingdom of Judah to **Babylon**, leaving only the poorest people behind to plant the fields.
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:20-11|[20-11]]]** About seventy years later, Cyrus, the king of the Persians, defeated **Babylon**.
