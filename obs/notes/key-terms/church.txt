====== church  iglesia     f ======

==== (church, Church) ====

**(iglesia, Iglesia)**
===== Definition: =====

**Definición**

The word, "church" refers to a group of people who believe in Jesus. When those believers are in a local area such as a city or a region, it is written as, "church." When it refers to believers everywhere, it is written as, "Church."

**La palabra "iglesia" se refiere a un grupo de personas que creen en Jesús. Cuando estos creyentes están en un área local como una ciudad o región,se escribe "iglesia." Cuando el término se refiere a creyentes en general, se escribe "Iglesia."**

   * The original meaning of the word for "church" in the Bible is "called out (to meet together)."
  * **El significado original de la palabra "iglesia" en la Biblia es: "ser llamados" (para reunirse)."**
  * "Church" could be translated as a "gathering together" or "assembly" of believers. The word "congregation" has a similar meaning. However, the believers are still the "church" even when they are not together.
  * **El termino "Iglesia" puede ser traducido como una "reunión" o "asamblea" de creyentes. La palabra "congregación" tiene un significado similar. Sin embargo, los creyentes siguen siendo la "Iglesia" aún cuando no esten juntos.**
  * In the Bible, the word "church" always refers to Christian believers, and never to a building.
  * **En la Biblia, la palabra "iglesia" siempre se refiere a creyentes Cristianos, y nunca a un edificio.**
  * The church meets together to pray, teach God's truth, and encourage each other to live in a way that is pleasing to God.
  * **La iglesia se reune para orar, enseñar la verdad acerca de Dios, y para motivar unos a otros a vivir en una forma que agrade a Dios.**
  * God also calls his people, the Church, to go out and teach the good message about Jesus.
  * **Dios también hace un llamado a su gente, la Iglesia, para que salgan a enseñar las buenas nuevas de Jesús.**

===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:43-12|[43-12]]]** About 3,000 people believed what Peter said and became disciples of Jesus. They were baptized and became part of the **church**  at Jerusalem.
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:46-09|[46-09]]]** Most of the people in Antioch were not Jews, but for the first time, very many of them also became believers. Barnabas and Saul went there to teach these new believers more about Jesus and to strengthen the **church**.
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:46-10|[46-10]]]** So the **church**  in Antioch prayed for Barnabas and Saul and placed their hands on them. Then they sent them off to preach the good news of Jesus in many other places.
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:47-13|[47-13]]]** The good news of Jesus kept spreading, and the **Church**  kept growing.
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:50-01|[50-01]]]** For almost 2,000 years, more and more people around the world have been hearing the good news about Jesus the Messiah. The **Church**  has been growing.
