====== punish   castigo ======

==== (to punish, punishment) ====

**(castigo, castigando)**

===== Definition: =====

**Definición**:

To punish someone is to cause that person to suffer a negative consequence for doing something wrong.

**Castigar a alguien es causar a una persona sufrimiento como la consecuencia negativa de hacer algo mal.**

   * God punished the Israelites when they disobeyed him, especially when they worshiped false gods.
  * **Dios castigaba a los israelitas cuando ellos le desobedecian, especialmente cuando ellos le rendían culto a dioses falsos.**
  * Sometimes punishment is intended to motivate a person to stop sinning, as when God punished the Israelites.
  * **Algunas veces los castigos tienen como intención motivar a una persona a dejar de pecar, como cuando Dios castigaba a los israelitas.**
  * Jesus was punished for all the evil things that every person has ever done. He received each person's punishment on himself even though he did nothing wrong and did not deserve that punishment.
  * **Jesús fue castigado por todas las cosas malas que toda persona haya hecho.  El recibio el castigo de cada persona sobre el mismo a pesar de que él no hizo nada malo y no merecía el castigo.**
===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:13-07|[13-07]]]** God also gave many other laws and rules to follow. If the people obeyed these laws, God promised that he would bless and protect them. If they disobeyed them, God would **punish**  them.
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:16-02|[16-02]]]** Because the Israelites kept disobeying God, he **punished**  them by allowing their enemies to defeat them.
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:19-16|[19-16]]]** The prophets warned the people that if they did not stop doing evil and start obeying God, then God would judge them as guilty, and he would **punish**  them.
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:48-06|[48-06]]]** Jesus was the perfect high priest because he took the **punishment**  for every sin that anyone has ever committed.
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:48-10|[48-10]]]** When anyone believes in Jesus, the blood of Jesus takes away that person's sin, and God's **punishment**  passes over him.
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:49-09|[49-09]]]** But God loved everyone in the world so much that he gave his only Son so that whoever believes in Jesus will not be **punished**  for his sins, but will live with God forever.
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:49-11|[49-11]]]** Jesus never sinned, but he chose to be **punished**  and die as the perfect sacrifice to take away your sins and the sins of every person in the world.
