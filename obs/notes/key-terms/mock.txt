====== mock burlarse ======

===== Definition: =====

===== Definición: =====

To deride or make fun of someone, especially in a cruel way.

Ridiculizar a alguien de una manera <font 14px line-height: 19.6000003814697px;/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>especialmente</font> cruel.

  * Mocking often involves taunting someone and laughing at them.
  * **Burla supone de insultar a alguien y reirse del.**
  * To mock someone is to pretend to honor them, but in a way that actually shows contempt for them.
  * **El burlarse de alguien es pretender honrarle pero todavía menospreciandole de alguna manera.**
  * A person can also mock someone by imitating that person's words or actions with the intent to embarrass them.
  * **Una persona también puede burlarse de alguien imitando las palabras o acciones <font 14px line-height: 19.6000003814697px;/inherit;;inherit;;inherit>de la persona</font> con el intento de avergonzarlo.**

===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:21-12|[21-12]]]** Isaiah prophesied that people would spit on, **mock**, and beat the Messiah.
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:39-05|[39-05]]]** The Jewish leaders all answered the high priest, "He deserves to die!" Then they blindfolded Jesus, spit on him, hit him, and **mocked**  him.
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:39-12|[39-12]]]** The soldiers whipped Jesus, and put a royal robe and a crown made of thorns on him. Then they **mocked**  him by saying, "Look, the King of the Jews!"
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:40-04|[40-04]]]** Jesus was crucified between two robbers. One of them **mocked**  Jesus, but the other said, "Do you have no fear of God?"
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:40-05|[40-05]]]** The Jewish leaders and the other people in the crowd **mocked**  Jesus. They said to him, "If you are the Son of God, come down from the cross and save yourself! Then we will believe you."
