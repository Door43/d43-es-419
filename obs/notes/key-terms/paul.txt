====== Paul Pablo ======

==== (Paul, Saul) ====

(Pablo, Saulo)

===== Facts: =====

**Datos:**

Paul was a leader of the early church who was sent by Jesus to take the good news to many other people groups.

**Pablo fue un líder de la iglesia primitiva que fue enviado por Jesús a llevar las Buenas Nuevas a la mayor cantidad de grupos de personas.**

   * Paul was a Jew who was born in the Roman city of Tarsus, and was therefore also a Roman citizen.
  * **Pablo era judío que nació en la ciudad romana de Tarsis, por lo cual era también un ciudadano judío.**
  * Paul was originally called by his Jewish name, Saul.
  * **Pablo era originalmente llamado por su nombre judío, Saulo.**
  * Saul became a Jewish religious leader and arrested Jews who became Christians because he did not believe in Jesus.
  * **Saulo se convirtió en un líder religioso judío y arrestaba a los judios que se convertían en cristianos por que el no creía en Jesús.**
  * Jesus came to Saul in a blinding light and told him to stop hurting Christians. Jesus then sent a Christian man to teach Saul, and Saul believed in Jesus.
  * **Jesús vino a Saulo en una luz enceguecedora y le dijo que no continuará hiriendo a los cristianos.  Jesús entonces envió un hombre cristiano a enseñar a Saulo, y Saulo creyó en Jesús.**
  * At first, Saul taught Jews about Jesus.
  * **Al principio, Saulo enseñaba a los judíos de Jesús.**
  * Later, God sent Saul to teach other people groups about Jesus in many Roman cities. Then he started using the Roman name, Paul.
  * **Luego, Dios envió a Saulo a enseñar a otros grupos de personas sobre Jesús en muchas ciudades romanas. Entonces el comenzó a usar su nombre romano, Pablo.**
  * Paul also wrote letters to encourage and teach Christians in various cities. Some of his letters are in the Bible.
  * **Pablo también escribio cartas motivando y enseñando a los cristianos en varias ciudades.  Algunas de sus cartas estan en la Biblia.**

===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:45-06|[45-06]]]** A young man named **Saul**  agreed with the people who killed Stephen and guarded their robes while they threw stones at him.
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:46-01|[46-01]]]****Saul**  was the young man who guarded the robes of the men who killed Stephen. He did not believe in Jesus, so he persecuted the believers.
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:46-02|[46-02]]]** While **Saul**  was on his way to Damascus, a bright light from heaven shone all around him, and he fell to the ground. **Saul**  heard someone say, "**Saul**! **Saul**! Why do you persecute me?"
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:46-05|[46-05]]]** So Ananias went to **Saul**, placed his hands on him, and said, "Jesus who appeared to you on your way here, sent me to you so that you can regain your sight and be filled with the Holy Spirit." **Saul**  immediately was able to see again, and Ananias baptized him.
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:46-06|[46-06]]]** Right away, **Saul**  began preaching to the Jews in Damascus, saying, "Jesus is the Son of God!"
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:46-09|[46-09]]]** Barnabas and **Saul**  went there (Antioch) to teach these new believers more about Jesus and to strengthen the church.
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:47-01|[47-01]]]** As **Saul**  traveled throughout the Roman Empire, he began to use his Roman name, "**Paul**."
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:47-14|[47-14]]]****Paul**  and other Christian leaders traveled to many cities, preaching and teaching people the good news about Jesus.
