====== Temple Templo ======

===== Facts: =====

===== Datos: =====

The Temple was a building where the Israelites offered sacrifices to God.

**El Templo era un edificio donde los israelitas ofrecían sacrificios a Dios.**

   * God referred to the Temple as his dwelling place where the people could come to him.
  * **Dios se refería al Templo como su lugar de morada donde la gente podía venir a El.**
  * Sometimes the term "Temple" refers to the whole Temple complex, including the courtyards that surrounded the main building.
  * **Algunas veces el término "Templo" se refiere a todo el complejo del Templo, incluyendo los patios que rodeaban el edificio principal.**
  * People came to the Temple building and its courtyards to pray, worship God and bring animals to sacrifice to God.
  * **Gente venían al Templo y a sus patios a orar, a adorar, a alabar a Dios y a traer animales para sacrificarle a Dios.**
  * The inner room of the Temple building, which had a thick curtain separating it from the rest of the Temple, could not be entered by anyone except the high priest.
  * **El cuarto interior del Templo tenía una cortina gruesa que lo separaba del resto del Templo y nadie aparte del sumo sacerdote podía entrar ahí.**
  * The original temple that Solomon built was destroyed by the Babylonians, but was rebuilt after the Exile.
  * **El templo original que Salomón construyó fue destruido por los babilonios pero fue reconstruido después del Exilio.**

===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:17-06|[17-06]]]** David wanted to build a **temple**  where all the Israelites could worship God and offer him sacrifices.
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:18-02|[18-02]]]** In Jerusalem, Solomon built the **Temple**  for which his father David had planned and gathered materials. Instead of at the Tent of Meeting, people now worshiped God and offered sacrifices to him at the **Temple**. God came and was present in the **Temple**, and he lived there with his people.
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:20-07|[20-07]]]** They (Babylonians) captured the city of Jerusalem, destroyed the **Temple**, and took away all the treasures.
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:20-13|[20-13]]]** When the people arrived in Jerusalem, they rebuilt the **Temple**  and the wall around the city of the city and the **Temple**.
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:25-04|[25-04]]]** Then Satan took Jesus to the highest point on the **Temple**  and said, "If you are the Son of God, throw yourself down, for it is written, 'God will command his angels to carry you so your foot does not hit a stone.'"
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:40-07|[40-07]]]** When he died, there was an earthquake and the large curtain that separated the people from the presence of God in the **Temple**  was torn in two, from the top to the bottom.
