====== cross //__cruz__ // ======

cruz

===== Definition: =====

**Definición:**

A cross is an upright wooden post stuck into the ground, with a horizontal wooden beam attached to it near the top.

**La cruz es un poste de madera erguido pegado en el suelo, con un viga de madera horizontal unida a la parte superior del poste.**

   * The Roman government would tie or nail criminals onto crosses and leave them there to die.
  * **El gobierno Romano ataba o clavaba a los criminales en las cruces y los dejaban allí para morir.**
  * Jesus was falsely accused and judged to be guilty of he did not commit. The Romans killed him on a cross.
  * **Jesús fue falsamente acusado y juzgado; declarado culpable de crímenes que el no cometió. Los Romanos lo mataron en la cruz.**
[See also: **[[:es-419:obs:notes:key-terms:crucify|crucify]]**, **[[:es-419:obs:notes:key-terms:rome|Rome]]**]

===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:40-01|[40-01]]]** After the soldiers mocked Jesus, they led him away to crucify him. They made him carry the **cross**  on which he would die.
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:40-02|[40-02]]]** The soldiers brought Jesus to a place called "the Skull" and nailed his arms and feet to the **cross**.
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:40-05|[40-05]]]** The Jewish leaders and the other people in the crowd mocked Jesus. They said to him, "If you are the Son of God, come down from the **cross**  and save yourself! Then we will believe you."
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:49-10|[49-10]]]** When Jesus died on the **cross**, he received your punishment.
  * **[[:es-419:obs:notes:frames:49-12|[49-12]]]** You must believe that Jesus is the Son of God, that he died on the **cross**  instead of you, and that God raised him to life again.
